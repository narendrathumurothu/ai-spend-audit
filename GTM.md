# GTM

## Target user

The exact target user is a SaaS or marketplace founder / head of engineering at an early-stage startup with 3–20 engineers who is already paying for AI tools. They are likely tracking tool spend manually and are open to cheaper AI infrastructure options.

## What they are doing right before they want this

- Searching for "AI tool cost optimization" or "Claude vs ChatGPT pricing".
- Reading Hacker News threads about startup burn and AI tooling.
- Browsing tweets from AI founders about expensive Copilot or OpenAI bills.
- Watching product launch newsletters on X or Indie Hackers.

## Where they hang out online

- X lists and threads focused on developer productivity and AI tooling.
- r/SaaS, r/Startups, and r/EntrepreneurRideAlong.
- Indie Hackers Slack and Makerpad communities.
- AI-focused Discord servers like Superteam and Buildspace.

## How to get the first 100 users with $0 paid budget

1. Post a short audit case study thread on X showing a real startup saving $1k/mo by switching plans.
2. Share the tool in targeted Slack communities for founders and engineering managers with a simple landing page that promises a "free AI spend health check".
3. Publish a concise post on Indie Hackers and Hacker News under a practical title like "We built a free AI spend audit for startups that are already paying for Copilot, Claude, or OpenAI."
4. Reach out to 5 founders in relevant X threads and offer a free audit in exchange for feedback.
5. Create a lightweight referral link: if a founder shares a saved audit page, both parties get a follow-up consultation priority.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex already has a sales motion for companies buying AI credits. The unfair advantage is turning that existing B2B funnel into a free audit lead magnet: every qualified audit becomes a warm lead that already understands the savings opportunity.

- 100 completed audits
