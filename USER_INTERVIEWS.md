# USER INTERVIEWS

## Interview 1 — F1, engineer at Gayan Data Solutions
- "I use Claude and Antigravity for both writing and coding." 
- "Antigravity is huge for me — I depend on it for fast code generation and writing documentation."
- "Claude is the tool I go to the most when I need something reliable quickly."

Most surprising thing: they use the same tools for both writing and coding, and they consider Antigravity the primary workhorse despite also using Claude.
What it changed: I added a `mixed` use case and stronger messaging that the audit can recommend the best tool for combined writing/code workflows.

## Interview 2 — F2, freelancer working with small teams
- "I use Claude, Gemini, and Cursor for scripts and coding, but Claude is the one I use hugely." 
- "I spend about $17 every month on Claude and I want to know if that spend is worth it."
- "I switch between tools depending on whether I am writing scripts or building product code."

Most surprising thing: even as a freelancer, the user had clear monthly spend and wanted a concrete cost benchmark rather than broad advice.
What it changed: I added better support for single-person and freelancer spend profiles, including a clear monthly-savings result and recommendation copy.

## Interview 3 — F3, geospatial engineer at TCS
- "I use AI tools a lot in my work, but my role is more geography-focused than pure software engineering."
- "I am curious how much I should be spending on the tools versus what I actually use for mapping and reports."
- "It feels risky to keep all these subscriptions without a second opinion on fit."

Most surprising thing: the user wanted the audit to validate tool fit for a non-engineering specialist role, not just pure coding teams.
What it changed: I added a clearer explanation that the audit is for team fit and workload type, not just raw tool price comparisons.
